## 3. Part II: Multi-Factorial Influences – Expanded Evidence Base

### 3.1 Psychological Factors

Premenstrual disorders may involve hormonal signaling and allopregnanolone/serotonin pathways.  
PMS affects ~25%; PMDD affects 2–5%.

### 3.2 Physiological Factors: Ovarian Aging Mechanisms

- Telomere shortening
- Oxidative stress
- Mitochondrial dysfunction
- Cellular senescence

### 3.3 Lifestyle Factors

Meta-analytic signals cited in corpus planning:
- Higher physical activity linked to lower menstrual pain (OR 0.67)
- Lower PMS scores in some analyses (OR 0.78)
- Lower cycle irregularity (OR 0.58)

### 3.4 Reproductive History: Breastfeeding and Period Return

Postpartum return of menses in lactation is variable and linked to feeding intensity and maternal factors.

## 4. Part III: Menstrual Disorders – Updated Clinical Guidelines

### 4.1 Premenstrual Disorders / PMDD (ACOG-style synthesis)

- DRSP symptom tracking and cyclicity confirmation for diagnosis.
- SSRI, certain COC, CBT, calcium are commonly cited options in summary form.

### 4.2 Endometriosis: Network Meta-Analysis Signals

- Lipiodol and laparoscopic interventions appear with high relative odds in pooled comparisons for infertility outcomes in selected analyses.
- Hormonal and procedural pain control options have differing ranking evidence.

### 4.3 Dysmenorrhea

- Global prevalence is high and heterogeneous.

### 4.4 PCOS and Fertility Outcomes

- Letrozole-stimulated FET shows reported advantages over artificial-cycle FET in selected pooled analyses (low-certainty overall).

## 5. Part IV: Special Life Stages

### 5.1 Perimenopause and Menopause (NHS Scotland synthesis)

- Menopause: typically no menses for 12 months.
- POI: persistent amenorrhea with elevated FSH in younger women.
- HRT timing/risk framing and route-specific safety patterns are summarized in this synthesis.

### 5.2 Postpartum and Breastfeeding

- Lactational amenorrhea duration is variable (months, rarely longer).
- Bleeding and cycle changes postpartum should be reviewed when heavy/irregular/painful.

## 6. Part V: Emerging Research
